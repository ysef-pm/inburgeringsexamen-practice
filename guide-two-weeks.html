What to practise two weeks before the inburgeringsexamen

A realistic 14-day plan — updated July 2026

Two weeks is not enough time to raise your Dutch a whole level — and the good news is you don't need to. In the final stretch, the biggest score gains come from exam technique and targeted repair, not new grammar. Here's how to spend the time.

First: stop doing these

  • Stop learning new vocabulary lists. Words learned this week won't be automatic under exam pressure. Consolidate what you have.
  • Stop passive studying. Watching Dutch TV with subtitles feels productive; it doesn't train exam tasks. Every session should produce something — an answer, a text, a recording.
  • Stop practising only your favourite skill. You pass per exam part. One weak part means a re-sit for that part, however strong the others are.

Week 1 — find and repair the gaps

Days 1–2Diagnose. Do one timed practice set per exam part (lezen, luisteren, KNM) and one writing + one speaking task. Note where points leak. If you want it structured, a readiness scorecard does this in one pass.
Days 3–5Attack the weakest part daily. 30 minutes on your worst skill, 15 on the rest, every day. For most people the weak part is spreken or schrijven — the productive skills.
Days 6–7Fix your repeat errors. Look at your week's mistakes; pick the three that recur (word order after "omdat", verb forms, forgotten capitals). Drill only those.

Week 2 — rehearse the real thing

Days 8–10Exam conditions only. Timed, one attempt, no dictionary, no replaying audio (the exam plays fragments once). This is where the one-time-listening reflex gets built.
Days 11–12Full dress rehearsal. One complete exam part per sitting, at the same time of day as your real exam if you can.
Day 13Light review. Re-do your three repair items, one short speaking recording, one short letter. Nothing new.
Day 14Rest and logistics. Seriously — cramming the last day trades points for anxiety.

Exam-day practicalities

  • Bring valid ID — the same document you registered with.
  • Arrive 30 minutes early; late arrival can mean forfeiting the slot and the fee.
  • Exams are on a computer at a DUO location; results typically arrive within weeks in Mijn Inburgering.
  • Failed one part? You re-book only that part — your passes stand.
